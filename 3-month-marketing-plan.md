# 3-Month LinkedIn & US Market Entry Plan
## Nipun Mehra + Neoflo

---

## The Big Picture

LinkedIn content alone will not get Neoflo US customers. This plan treats content as one part of a three-part system:

1. **Content** — builds trust with buyers before they ever talk to you
2. **Outreach** — warms up the right people directly
3. **Network activation** — turns VC relationships and warm intros into conversations

All three run simultaneously. Content without outreach is slow. Outreach without content has no credibility. Neither works without activating the existing network.

---

## 3-Month Goals

| Month | Content Goal | Business Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 1** | Build Nipun's credibility with US buyers | 50 targeted connections added per week |
| **Month 2** | Introduce proof, begin direct outreach | 5–10 discovery calls booked |
| **Month 3** | Convert attention into pipeline | 2–3 active US prospects in conversation |

## Target Audience — US Market

**Primary Buyers (Decision Makers):**
| Role | Why They Care |
|---|---|
| CFOs at Series B–D startups (50–500 employees) | Scaling fast, back-office is breaking |
| VP Finance / Controllers at mid-market companies ($10M–$200M revenue) | Paying BPO firms, frustrated with results |
| COOs at PE-backed companies | Post-acquisition ops consolidation |
| Heads of Shared Services | Currently buying BPO — Neoflo is the alternative |

**Secondary (Referral Sources):**
- VCs / investors at Peak XV, Lightspeed, Alter Global portfolio companies
- Boutique finance consultants
- CFO peer networks and communities

**Geography:** United States — primary. Southeast Asia and India secondary.

# MONTH 1 — Weeks 1–4
## Theme: Plant Seeds. No Selling.

**Goal: Make US CFOs/COOs think "this person understands my world." Zero product pushing.**

### Content — Month 1

**Nipun: 2 posts/week | Neoflo: 1 post/week**

| Week | Nipun Post 1 | Nipun Post 2 | Neoflo Post |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Contrarian stat — *"65% of finance team time goes to tasks that shouldn't need a human. Nobody is solving this."* | Short list — *"3 signs your back-office is about to break under growth"* | Company belief statement |
| **2** | Industry insight — *"The real reason BPO companies can't automate themselves"* | Personal story — *"What I learned shutting down a $250M revenue company"* | Finance stat — shareable data point |
| **3** | Contrarian — *"Everyone's talking about AI replacing jobs. CFOs are asking a different question."* | Buyer education — *"4 questions CFOs should ask before any automation contract"* | Repost Nipun week 3 with company context |
| **4** | Market thesis — *"The $1T wiped from BPO stocks isn't a crisis. It's a signal."* | Pattern interrupt — *"AI won't replace your finance team. It'll expose who's been doing real work."* | Team spotlight — Kaustav Ray |

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 1

**LinkedIn Connection Building (daily, 15 min)**
- 10 connection requests per day to US CFOs, COOs, VP Finance, Controllers
- Personal note — not a pitch: *"Following your work in finance ops — would love to connect."*
- Target: 50–70 new relevant connections per week

**VC Network Activation (week 2)**
- Email 3–5 partners at Peak XV, Lightspeed, Alter Global
- Ask: *"Do you have US portfolio companies where the CFO is struggling with back-office scaling? Would love a warm intro."*

**Daily Content Engagement (10 min)**
- Comment thoughtfully on posts by US CFOs and COOs
- 2–3 sentence comments — not "great post"
- Gets Nipun's name in front of buyers before they see his content

### Metrics — Month 1
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Profile visits per week | Growing week over week |
| New relevant connections | 200–280 by end of month |
| Post saves | 2–3 per post minimum |
| DMs received | Any — even 1 is a signal |
| Comments from CFO/COO titles | Track titles of who engages |

# MONTH 2 — Weeks 5–8
## Theme: Build Proof. Begin Outreach.

**Goal: Shift from "interesting perspective" to "these people actually deliver results."**

### Content — Month 2

**Nipun: 2 posts/week | Neoflo: 1 post/week**

| Week | Nipun Post 1 | Nipun Post 2 | Neoflo Post |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5** | Case story #1 (anonymized) — invoice exception problem solved | CFO pain — *"Month-end close shouldn't take 12 days"* | Result post — *"We helped a finance team cut month-end close from 12 days to 3"* |
| **6** | Credibility anchor — *"What I learned from Sequoia portfolio companies about back-office"* | Case story #2 (different use case) | *"What we do differently from traditional BPO and software-only tools"* |
| **7** | Industry analysis — *"Goldman needed Anthropic on-site for 6 months for 2 workflows. Here's what that means."* | Honest take — *"The back-office is where AI projects go to die. Here's why."* | LinkedIn poll — *"CFOs: what's your biggest back-office headache right now?"* |
| **8** | Repeat best-performing Month 1 format with new topic | Process education — *"What actually happens inside an automation project vs. what vendors promise"* | Product explanation — how Neoflo works in plain English |

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 2

**Direct Outreach Sequence (week 5 onward)**
- 20 outreach sequences per week
- Expected response rate: 10–15%

Sequence:
- *Day 1:* Connection request with personal note
- *Day 5:* First message after acceptance — reference their company, no pitch
- *Day 12:* Follow-up referencing a specific post

**Podcast / Speaking Outreach (week 6)**
Target podcasts:
- The CFO Podcast
- CFO Thought Leader
- The Modern CFO
- VC-hosted webinars for portfolio CFOs

Pitch angle: *"Founder who ran finance ops at scale ($250M revenue), backed by Peak XV/Lightspeed, building AI-first BPO. Can speak to what the $300B BPO disruption means for CFOs."*

### Metrics — Month 2
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Outreach sequences started | 20 per week |
| Response rate | 10–15% |
| Discovery calls booked | 5–10 by end of month |
| Inbound DMs from content | Track which topics drive them |
| Podcast pitches sent | 10–15 |

# MONTH 3 — Weeks 9–12
## Theme: Convert. Scale What Works.

**Goal: Turn trust and conversations into active US prospects. Content speaks directly to the decision maker with soft CTAs.**

### Content — Month 3

**Nipun: 2 posts/week | Neoflo: 1 post/week**

| Week | Nipun Post 1 | Nipun Post 2 | Neoflo Post |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9** | COO angle — *"5 back-office problems that look like people problems but aren't"* | Market thesis — *"The $300B BPO industry is being restructured. Here's who wins."* | Hiring/culture post — signals US commitment |
| **10** | Direct buyer — *"If you're a CFO evaluating automation, here's the framework I'd use before talking to any vendor"* | Best-performing Month 1–2 format with new topic | US market announcement post |
| **11** | Aspirational — *"What the best-run finance teams at Series C companies have in common"* | Objection-handling — address #1 concern from discovery calls | Case story #3 — most compelling result |
| **12** | Soft CTA — *"If your team is still doing [X] manually, DM me. Happy to show you what it looks like solved."* | Reflection — *"3 months building in the US market. Here's what I've learned."* | Direct CTA — *"We're taking on 3 new US clients. Here's who it's a fit for."* |

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 3

**Scale Outreach on What Works**
- Use only the message that got the best response rate in Month 2
- Reference most effective content post in outreach

**Convert Discovery Calls**
- Follow up within 24 hours of every call
- Send one relevant case story that addresses their specific problem
- Ask for second call with a clear agenda

**LinkedIn Live / Roundtable (week 11)**
- Host: *"What CFOs at Series B–D companies are getting wrong about back-office automation"*
- Invite 5–10 connections from the past 2 months
- Low pressure, high value, direct access to qualified buyers

### Metrics — Month 3
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Active US prospects in pipeline | 2–3 minimum |
| Discovery calls completed (months 2–3 total) | 10–15 |
| Proposals or follow-up conversations | 3–5 |
| Inbound DMs per week | 2–4 by week 12 |
| Podcast appearances | 1–2 completed |

## What NOT to Track

- Total impressions
- Like count
- Follower growth week-to-week
- Comments and engagement from other founders

These feel good. They do not predict revenue.
