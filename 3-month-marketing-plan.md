# 3-Month LinkedIn & US Market Entry Plan
## Nipun Mehra + Neoflo

---

## The Big Picture

LinkedIn content alone will not get Neoflo US customers. This plan treats content as one part of a three-part system:

1. **Content** — builds trust with buyers before they ever talk to you
2. **Outreach** — warms up the right people directly
3. **Network activation** — turns VC relationships and warm intros into conversations

All three run simultaneously. Content without outreach is slow. Outreach without content has no credibility. Neither works without activating the existing network.

---

## Guiding Principles

- **Focus over variety** — fewer content types done consistently beats many content types done inconsistently
- **No video or webinars until Month 3** — high effort, low return without an existing US audience
- **Product posts belong in Month 3** — not before trust is established
- **Every post must answer:** *"Does this make a US CFO or COO more likely to trust Nipun and Neoflo?"*
- **Every activity should put Nipun in front of US CFOs and COOs** — if it doesn't, deprioritize it

# MONTH 1 — Weeks 1–4
## Theme: Plant Seeds. No Selling.

**Goal: Make US CFOs/COOs think "this person understands my world." Zero product pushing. No CTAs.**

Every post this month plants one seed toward the answer:
> *"What does a US CFO need to believe before they would ever buy Neoflo?"*

### Content Calendar — Month 1

| Week | Nipun Tuesday | Nipun Thursday | Neoflo Wednesday |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Contrarian stat — *"65% of finance team time goes to tasks that shouldn't need a human. Nobody is solving this."* | Short list — *"3 signs your back-office is about to break under growth"* | Company belief statement — what Neoflo stands for |
| **2** | Industry insight — *"The real reason BPO companies can't automate themselves"* | Personal credibility — *"What I learned shutting down a $250M revenue company"* | Shareable stat post |
| **3** | Contrarian — *"Everyone's talking about AI replacing jobs. CFOs are asking a different question."* | Buyer education — *"4 questions CFOs should ask before any automation contract"* | Repost Nipun week 3 Thursday + added company context |
| **4** | Market thesis — *"The $1T wiped from BPO stocks isn't a crisis. It's a signal."* | Pattern interrupt — *"AI won't replace your finance team. It'll expose who's been doing real work."* | Kaustav Ray employee expertise post |

**If Nipun attends any event or webinar this month:** replace that week's Thursday post with an event recap.

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 1

**LinkedIn Connection Building (daily, 15 min)**
- 10 connection requests per day to US CFOs, COOs, VP Finance, Controllers
- Personal note — not a pitch: *"Following your work in finance ops — would love to connect."*
- Target: 50–70 new relevant connections per week

**VC Network Activation (week 2)**
- Email 3–5 partners at Peak XV, Lightspeed, Alter Global
- Ask specifically: *"Do you have US portfolio companies where the CFO is struggling with back-office scaling? Would love a warm intro."*

**Daily Content Engagement (10 min)**
- Comment thoughtfully on 3–5 posts by US CFOs and COOs
- 2–3 sentence comments — not "great post" — add a real perspective or question
- Gets Nipun's name in front of buyers before they ever see his content

### Metrics — Month 1
| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Profile visits per week | Growing week over week |
| New relevant connections | 200–280 by end of month |
| Post saves | 2–3 per post minimum |
| DMs received | Any — even 1 is a signal |
| Comments from CFO/COO titles | Track titles of who engages |

### Month 1 Checkpoint
- Which posts got the most saves and shares?
- Has anyone DM'd about their own back-office problem?
- Which connection request note got the best acceptance rate?
- Are any VC intros in motion?

**The answers determine what to double down on in Month 2.**

# MONTH 2 — Weeks 5–8
## Theme: Build Proof. Begin Outreach.

**Goal: Shift from "interesting perspective" to "these people actually deliver results." First direct outreach begins.**

### Content Calendar — Month 2

| Week | Nipun Tuesday | Nipun Thursday | Neoflo Wednesday |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5** | Case story #1 (anonymized) — invoice exception problem solved | CFO pain — *"Month-end close shouldn't take 12 days. Here's what's actually causing the delay."* | Result post — *"We helped a finance team cut month-end close from 12 days to 3"* |
| **6** | Credibility anchor — *"What I learned from Sequoia portfolio companies about back-office ops"* | Case story #2 (different use case — vendor payments or customer ops) | *"What we do differently from traditional BPO and software-only tools"* |
| **7** | Industry analysis — *"Goldman needed Anthropic on-site for 6 months for 2 workflows. Here's what that means for every CFO evaluating AI."* | Honest take — *"The back-office is where AI projects go to die. Here's why."* | LinkedIn poll — *"CFOs: what's your biggest back-office headache right now?"* |
| **8** | Repeat best-performing Month 1 format with new topic | Process education — *"What actually happens inside an automation project vs. what vendors promise"* | Operations team employee post |

**Event recap rule:** If Nipun attends any event, replace that week's Thursday post with a recap.

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 2

**Direct Outreach Sequence (week 5 onward — 20 sequences per week)**

*Day 1 — Connection request:*
> *"Hi [Name] — I've been following [company] and the challenges finance teams face at your stage. Building something in this space and would love to connect."*

*Day 5 — First message after acceptance:*
> *"Thanks for connecting. I noticed [specific thing about their role or company]. We've been working with finance teams going through similar scaling challenges — would love to hear how you're thinking about it. No agenda, just a conversation."*

*Day 12 — Follow-up if no reply:*
> *"Dropping this back to the top — I wrote about [specific post topic] last week that might be relevant to what you're dealing with. Happy to share or just chat."*

Expected response rate: 10–15%

**Podcast / Speaking Outreach (week 6)**
- Target: The CFO Podcast, CFO Thought Leader, The Modern CFO, VC-hosted webinars for portfolio CFOs
- Send 10–15 pitches
- Pitch angle: *"Founder who ran finance ops at scale ($250M revenue), backed by Peak XV/Lightspeed, building AI-first BPO. Can speak to what the $300B BPO disruption means for CFOs."*

**CA Events Research (week 6–7)**
- Identify 2–3 CFO or finance ops conferences in California
- Avoid general AI/tech events — wrong audience
- Target: CFO Summit, finance transformation conferences, PE portfolio company events
- Register and pitch for a speaking slot

# MONTH 3 — Weeks 9–12
## Theme: Convert. Scale What Works.

**Goal: Turn trust and conversations built in months 1–2 into active US prospects. Introduce product context. Soft CTAs begin.**

### Content Calendar — Month 3

| Week | Nipun Tuesday | Nipun Thursday | Neoflo Wednesday |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9** | COO angle — *"5 back-office problems that look like people problems — but aren't"* | Market thesis — *"The $300B BPO industry is being restructured. Here's who wins."* | Behind the process — what Neoflo actually does day to day |
| **10** | Direct CFO buyer — *"If you're evaluating back-office automation, here's the framework I'd use before talking to any vendor"* | Best-performing Month 1–2 format with new topic | US market announcement — Neoflo is building here |
| **11** | Problem → solution post — first post introducing a specific Neoflo capability in context of a buyer problem | Objection-handling — address the #1 concern heard in discovery calls | Case story #3 — most compelling result to date |
| **12** | Soft CTA — *"If your team is still doing [X] manually, DM me. Happy to show you what it looks like solved."* | Reflection — *"3 months building in the US market. Here's what I've learned."* | Direct CTA — *"We're taking on 3 new US clients. Here's who it's a fit for."* |

**CA event attendance (week 10–11):** If attending a CA event, that week's Thursday post becomes an on-site recap.

### Product Content in Month 3
Week 11 is the first time a specific Neoflo capability is introduced — and only in the context of a buyer problem. Follow the product post formula:
1. Name the problem
2. Show why it's hard
3. Validate what they've tried
4. Introduce what Neoflo does differently
5. Show the outcome

### Off-Platform Actions — Month 3

**Scale Outreach on What Works**
- Use only the message that got the best response rate in Month 2
- Reference the best-performing content post in outreach messages

**Convert Discovery Calls**
- Follow up within 24 hours of every call
- Send one case story that directly addresses their specific problem
- Ask for a second call with a clear agenda

**CFO Invite-Only Roundtable — Week 11 (if pipeline is ready)**
- Small dinner or roundtable for 10–15 US CFO/COO connections built over 2 months
- Location: San Francisco or Los Angeles
- Format: No pitch. Nipun facilitates a conversation. Topic: *"What back-office automation actually looks like when it works."*
- Send invites in week 9
- Post a recap in week 12: *"Had 12 CFOs in a room in SF. Here's the one thing every single one said."*

**Only host the roundtable if you have enough warm US connections to fill the room with the right people. If not, skip and use the time for outreach.**

## April — Merger Announcement

The 3-month plan ends in March. The merger lands in April. This is a separate content moment — not part of the 3-month calendar — but it must be planned in advance.

**Why timing matters:** 3 months of consistent content means the merger announcement lands to a warm, credible US audience instead of going out cold.

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| **Month 1–3** | Build Neoflo brand independently. Do not reference the merger in content. |
| **2 weeks before April** | Draft Nipun's personal merger announcement post — why, what it means, what the combined company does |
| **Announcement week** | Nipun posts long-form personal piece. Neoflo page posts official announcement. Coordinate both same day. |
| **Week after** | Follow-up content — what the merger means specifically for US back-office buyers |

**Employee content note:** Do not feature employees in roles that may change post-merger. Stick to Kaustav Ray and core operations team members through March.
