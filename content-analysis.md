## Core Issues

### Issue 1 — Wrong Audience
Current content speaks to **founders, VCs, and tech people.** The actual buyer — US CFOs, COOs, VP Finance, and Heads of Shared Services — is not being targeted. None of the posts are written from the buyer's perspective.

### Issue 2 — Leading With the Product
Almost every post ends with "this is why we built Neoflo." The viewer has not been given a reason to care before the product is introduced. Per proven content frameworks, the sequence should be:
> Hook → Curiosity → Emotion → Product

Nipun skips the first three and jumps to the fourth.

### Issue 3 — No Repeatable Format
Posts vary in structure and style every time. High-performing content is built on **repeatable formats** — once something works, you run it again with a different topic. There is no format consistency.

### Issue 4 — No Proof
Despite operating for over a year, there are **no case studies, anonymized client results, or before/after stories** on either page. This is the most important missing content for enterprise B2B buyers.

### Issue 5 — Company Page Is Underutilized
The Neoflo company page has no consistent voice, no clear value proposition communicated through content, and no buyer-specific posts. When a US CFO visits the page after reading Nipun's content, there is nothing to reinforce trust.

### Issue 6 — Vanity Metrics Focus
Success has been measured by impressions and likes — both of which have low correlation to enterprise B2B leads. The real metrics are: **DMs received, post saves, profile visits, and discovery calls booked.**

### Issue 7 — No US Market Presence
All content, events, and networking references are Singapore, India, and Southeast Asia. The US market entry has not been reflected in content, outreach, or positioning yet.

## What Is Working

- Nipun's **personal credibility** (Sequoia, Ula, Stanford/Wharton) is a strong trust signal that is underused
- His **market analysis posts** (BPO Innovator's Dilemma, Goldman/Anthropic) get the most engagement and are closest to the right format
- The **core thesis is correct and differentiated** — AI-first BPO with outcome SLAs is a real gap in the market
- Backed by **Peak XV, Lightspeed, Alter Global** — strong signal for US enterprise buyers that is not being leveraged in content
