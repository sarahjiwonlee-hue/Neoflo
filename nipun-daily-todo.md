# Nipun's Daily LinkedIn & US Market Entry To-Do
## Total time required: ~60 minutes per day

---

## Every Day (Monday–Friday)

### Morning — 20 minutes

**1. Engage with US buyers (10 min)**
- Open LinkedIn
- Search feed for posts by: CFO, COO, VP Finance, Controller, Head of Finance Operations
- Leave 3–5 thoughtful comments on their posts
- Comments must be 2–3 sentences — a real response to what they said
- Do NOT comment: "Great post!" or "So true!" — add a perspective or ask a question
- Why: Gets your name in front of buyers before they ever see your content

**2. Send connection requests (10 min)**
- Send 10 connection requests to US-based CFOs, COOs, VP Finance
- Search terms to use: "CFO" + Series B/C/D, "VP Finance" + SaaS, "Controller" + tech startup
- Company size filter: 50–500 employees
- Personal note to attach (copy and adapt):
  > *"Hi [Name] — I've been building in the back-office AI space and follow operators like you closely. Would love to connect."*
- Target: 10 per day, 50 per week

---

### Midday — 10 minutes

**3. Check and respond to DMs**
- Reply to every DM within the same day — response speed signals how you treat clients
- If someone engages with a post or asks a question: respond with a specific, helpful answer
- If a connection request was accepted: send a first message (see outreach sequence below)

---

### End of Day — 30 minutes (writing days only)

**4. Write and schedule posts — Tuesday and Thursday only**
- One post per writing session
- Use approved formats (see content plan):
  - Contrarian stat + POV
  - Short list (3–5 items)
  - Mini case story (problem → diagnosis → outcome)
  - Pattern interrupt
  - Direct buyer education
- First line = the hook. Rewrite it 3 times before publishing. It determines if anyone reads the rest.
- Do not post and disappear — stay on LinkedIn for 30 minutes after posting to reply to every comment
- Why: Early engagement signals boost distribution

## Weekly Tasks

### Every Monday
- [ ] Review last week's post performance — which got saves, DMs, profile visits
- [ ] Note what worked and why — use that format again next week
- [ ] Check connection request acceptance rate — if below 30%, adjust your note
- [ ] Identify 3 US CFO/COO posts to comment on this week

### Every Wednesday
- [ ] Follow up on all pending outreach sequences (Day 5 and Day 12 follow-ups)
- [ ] Check if any VC intro requests need a follow-up email
- [ ] Review any DMs that came in from content — respond and qualify

### Every Friday
- [ ] Record the week's metrics:
  - DMs received this week
  - Post saves this week
  - Profile visits this week
  - Connections accepted this week
  - Discovery calls booked this week
- [ ] Write next week's two post drafts (can be rough — edit Monday)

## Outreach Sequence (Month 2 onward)

Once you have 4 weeks of content on your profile, begin direct outreach.

**Day 1 — Connection request**
> *"Hi [Name] — I've been following [company] and the challenges finance teams face at your stage. Building something in this space and would love to connect."*

**Day 5 — First message (after acceptance)**
> *"Thanks for connecting [Name]. I noticed [specific thing about their role or company]. We've been working with finance teams going through similar scaling challenges — would love to hear how you're thinking about it. No agenda, just a conversation."*

**Day 12 — Follow-up if no reply**
> *"Dropping this back to the top — I wrote about [specific topic] last week that might be relevant to what you're dealing with. Happy to share or just chat."*

**If they respond:** Move to a 20-minute call. Not a demo. A discovery conversation.
- Ask: What does your current back-office process look like?
- Ask: Where does it break down?
- Ask: What have you tried?
- Do NOT pitch until they've described the problem in their own words

## What to STOP Doing

| Stop | Why |
|---|---|
| Checking like counts | Likes don't predict revenue |
| Reposting without adding commentary | Adds no value to your audience |
| Posting and immediately leaving LinkedIn | You lose early comment momentum |
| Generic connection requests with no note | Low acceptance rate |
| Pitching Neoflo in the first message | Kills the conversation before it starts |
| Posting on weekends | Low B2B engagement |
| Writing posts about AI trends with no buyer angle | Speaks to founders, not CFOs |

## Signs the Strategy Is Working

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| A CFO or COO DMs you after a post | Content is reaching the right people |
| Someone saves your post | They plan to come back — high intent |
| A connection request is accepted quickly | Your note resonated |
| Someone forwards your post to a colleague | Earned distribution — strong signal |
| A VC says "I have someone you should talk to" | Network activation is working |
| A discovery call gets a second meeting | You're solving a real problem for them |

## The One Rule

> Post for the CFO who is reading quietly and will never like or comment — but might DM you on a Tuesday morning when their month-end close is a disaster.

Everything else is noise.
